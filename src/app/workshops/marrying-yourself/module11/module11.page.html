LOVER - SHARINGS FROM MEN...

The "Lover Archetype" feels connected to the Universe, which I experience as the “Great Mystery”. I often experience the lover archetype through my senses, which are heightened and feel awake. This is both a blessing and it can also be a burden — if not tamed with boundaries.

The lover can easily be seduced, if not grounded in the KING. The boundaries from the King are critically important to how I am able to manage all that I feel — versus being chaotic and boundless with my feelings and senses.

As the lover, I have learned how supremely impactful it is for me to be clear and committed to my boundaries, in alignment with the King archetype.

If not guided by The King archetype, "I" the Lover, will chase the intensity of all that I feel and sense and then be left with wanting more. I will therefore find myself creating messy situations, with lots of drama because I'm looking for the lover in me to be met outside of myself (which is insatiable).

As I mature and season as a man, I experience my lover archetype self loving my-self unconditionally and therefore, grounding in my primary relationship with myself as first and foremost.

I validate my lover by validating my authenticity.

The lover seeks connection and reciprocation. He may or may not get that reciprocation from others so therefore he must not depend on those outside of himself to validate his lover self as ONLY he can.

Feeling and sensing can lead to hurt and pain but NOT numbness. Better to feel than to be numb. Even in the hurt and pain, I find myself alive and honoring of the “Great Mystery,” which I experience as within everyone and everything.

I also recognize that I must be kind to myself and love myself along the way, all the while being kind and loving to all others. A Win Win! This is The Way of The Lover archetype as I live it.

-Jeffree S. Gars
LCSW Psychotherapist/Counselor, DC Doctor of Chiropractic

WARRIOR - SHARINGS FROM MEN...

Although I can, of course, see elements of all four archetypes in myself, there is no question that the Warrior in me is the strongest and most prominent.

[I know - kind of odd for a 65 year old peace-nik who hasn't been in a physical fight since 5th grade!]

The Warrior spirit in me is what has kept me alive: the willingness to go through (sometimes intense) internal discomfort in service to the Greater Missions of Acceptance and Kindness and Inclusion.

If I've got any "super-powers", it's telling the truth even when it's not comfortable, and NEVER GIVING UP. Every time life knocks me down emotionally (which it has a tendency to do at times), the Warrior in me eventually gets me back up and back on Mission. It doesn't matter how wounded I feel, or how embarrassed. Something in me just will not allow me to give up on, or to betray, my Mission to support the freer flow of Love and Kindness in this world.

All these skills were essential, for example, to my getting through graduate school: pushing through endless tasks and assignments and tests and challenges to fulfill the Mission of becoming capable of helping others.

Far more challenging than that, though, surviving and healing from my shitty childhood has required every bit of Warrior energy I could find in myself. Originally, I put my Warrior energy to work building and maintaining a fortress to protect my heart (i.e., "what is sacred"). Then, in my twenties and thirties, I discovered that this very same fortress I had worked so hard to build was now directly in the way of my ability to experience love. So now much of my Warrior energy is assigned to the task of actively "de-structuring" the walls and motes and booby traps of this fortress that no longer serves me.

Said another way, it took drawing on Warrior energy to erect and maintain my inner protection mechanisms in order to survive as a kid. And now, in order to thrive as an adult, I am applying this same Warrior energy to the task of deconstructing the inner prison that my fortress eventually became.

Nevertheless, the Mission has always been the same: To do whatever it takes to protect and clear the way for Love to flow more powerfully and freely through my own true heart.

One of my current growing edges as a warrior is self-protection. I deeply value courageous vulnerability, the strength it takes to tell the truth about one's insides, even when it's not flattering to one's "image" or conducive to one's "standing." I deeply value the courage it takes to honestly acknowledge genuine weakness or woundedness or need -- and I deeply value the strength it takes to ask for (much less receive!) real help from others. Lately, however, I'm coming to realize that I sometimes take it too far, and lead with "courageous vulnerability" to the point where I make myself vulnerable by the dictionary definition of the word, i.e., "susceptible to attack."

My childhood did not teach me how to stand up for myself, or even that I was worth standing up for. My dad's best (and no doubt well-intentioned) advice to me about dealing with bullies was to do whatever it took to AVOID them — cross the street, walk the other way, pretend you don't notice them, don't make eye contact, get small and play dead. "Don't make trouble."

Obviously, these are very "unwarriorlike" approaches to protecting what is sacred (i.e., one's own true self). Much of who I am today can be understood as a replay or a reaction to these powerfully fear-based, disempowering early learnings. I continue to extricate myself from this "trance of fear" on a daily basis.

Another growing edge for me as a Warrior these days is discipline. Many times in my life I have been remarkably disciplined — clearly even over-disciplined (e.g., working out so much that I lost muscle mass). In recent years, however, I can see that I have swung too far the other way again. I have allowed myself to get too comfortable with feeling comfortable. The Warrior in me knows that it's time to tighten things up again — to expect more from myself, to take better care of myself. Hopefully this time around I will find a better balance point between refusing comfort and indulging it.

Whether I had the words or not, my longstanding self-proclaimed Mission has always been to be as open a conduit for the free flow of Love & Light into this world as I can possibly be.

By "self-proclaimed", I don't so much mean to imply that I CHOSE this Mission — like one's sexual preferences, it's more like I discovered that this simply IS my Mission. It really doesn't feel "optional" to me.

If you think about it, there is an obvious irony and seeming contradiction in being a "Warrior of and for Love." How can the skills of a warrior possibly be applied to "protecting" Love & Kindness?

This are my ongoing dilemmas — How do I stand up strong for kindness? What must I vanquish in the name of Love? Since the principles of Acceptance, Kindness and Inclusion are my "King", by definition however I stand up, and whatever I see fit to vanquish, I must find a way to do so with an intention to do no harm, and to only destroy that which gets in the way of the free flow of Love & Kindness & Connectedness.

On the shadow side, my inner Warrior itches to DO SOMETHING to "fight" the forces of racism, of patriarchy, of cruelty and exclusion, of hypocrisy and blind obedience to false authority. The danger here, as always, is getting myself stuck in the mode of "looking for a fight" (e.g., "doom-scrolling") — and/or giving in to "hating the haters" (thus becoming exactly what I claim to be fighting "against").

At the core of my Warrior Heart, I know Love cannot be served by hating (or fearing) ANYONE (including "me").

So it seems my Core Warrior Mission at the present time is finding the wisdom to not give in to hate or fear or shame -- and the courage, instead, to notice and stand up for what is beautiful in all of us (yes, including "me"!).

-Tony Rooney
July, 2022

Venturing into Sacred Space | Archetype of the Magician

MAGICIAN - SHARINGS FROM MEN...
“I want to know if you can disappoint another to be true to yourself. If you can bear the accusation of betrayal and not betray your own soul. If you can be faithless and therefore trustworthy.”
Oriah Mountain Dreamer The Invitation

I am a magician.

On many different levels.

I'll show you the most obvious demonstration by a magic trick.

By careful manipulation, deception, misdirection and story - I entertain you.

It sets your brain whirling - how?

And we don't tell you. We want you to enjoy and to struggle with how and why.

In my life - I have been a magician, a photographer, a priest, a psychotherapist, a father.

I attempted social change and failed - I have been a 'wrench in the gears' - subversive, a skeptic, a counter cultural irritant, a political activist and at present - I am a change agent.

As a part of those things - necessary to the doing of those things - I have also been a thief, a liar, a manipulator, an opportunist, an adulterer...fueling my shadow - and its impact... my self/ego destruction, relationship abuse, and voluntary defrocking.

---------------------------
The magician watches and thinks. (observing ego)

He observes and makes sense of (too much) information. (knowing what others don't)

He helps people see and make decisions. (manipulation)
---------------------------

I was a lonely child - a latchkey kid - alone. I didn't know at the time - I was an abused child.

This forged an introversion. I wanted to be liked. I was sweet and funny. I was and am mischievous. I love girls and animals.

I was and am an observer - of what was around me.

I saw racism. hypocrisy. injustice. exclusion.

I don't like people.

And I saw inclusion, art, helpers, love and embrace.

I love people.

I see the darkness of the gothic south, the lies of the church and white people, the manipulation in politics for personal gain.

My generation (the boomers) wanted to be different. We all saw what was. We intended to get rid of the "man" and replace it with love, peace and rock and roll (and drugs). We sold out.

I see how people can be brought into the sacred - in nature, a pew, a therapist couch, a photograph - in an embrace.

At my best - I saved and restored life, found lost souls, included the excluded, saw beauty and loved big.

At my worst - I was not aware of the 'same time' destruction my shadow wrought.

I am at present - humbled, quiet, and seeking to restructure how I am to use my power for good. To control my grandiosity and keep my ego deconstructed. I want to recover and restore. I want to love big.

Be a magician.

Observe, know, manipulate for good.

Know your shadow.

-Thomas Watson Query
June 19, 2022
