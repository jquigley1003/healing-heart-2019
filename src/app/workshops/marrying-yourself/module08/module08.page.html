PRACTICES FOR BEING PRESENT, NOW WOW

~~ Experiment with intensifying your senses. Choose one at a time and intensify it. Experience your world through the gift of this sense. For example, seeing.. experience seeing... the gift of seeing...and drink in what you see, take it in. Experience the power of seeing. Go on to hearing, kinesthetic, olfactory, taste. Experience being present through your senses. Sometimes this is best done in Nature, either taking a walk or sitting. If you are walking, practice tuning in through one sense at a time with each block you walk. If you are sitting, change channels of your senses every few minutes. Write about your experience.

~~ Now, Wow... As you attune into the now, each now... There is the wow... Practice being in the wow. Practice seeing the wow in everything. Remember, to wow is to acknowledge someone, to honor them, to celebrate them, to truly experience the wonder of them. Practice being with, “Hello. I wow you!” At the end of your day, write about your experience for five minutes of being in the now wow for five minutes.

PRACTICES FOR BREATHING, SOFTENING, ALLOWING, OPENING

~~ Practice going outside and truly breathing deep and long. Allow the inhale to be like powerful ingesting of Prana, and allow your exhale to be long and go all the way before you inhale. Every system in the body relies on oxygen. As you breathe deep and long, you allow for greater mental cognition and clarity, digestion, sleep, improved immunity response, and deeper sleep. Breathe deeper and longer, you are freer from stress. Breathing consciously and fully is one of the most loving things you can do for yourself. It is the beginning point for all else. It is the opening. It is the yes. It is the claiming of your birthright to live and take up space. It informs your system that you know you are worthy and have a right to drink of life energy fully and shamelessly. It is your willingness to be alive.

~~ Practice this 4-7-8 breathing technique for ten minutes. Set your timer. Then write about what you noticed about your experience. How do you practice 4-7-8?

1. Breathe in through your nose to the count of four.
2. Hold the breath to the count of seven.
3. Exhale through your mouth to the count of eight.

~~ Now, lets add the mantra to it and see how that is for you. With the four count inhale, allow an Om to resonate within. Hold for seven with Namah. Exhale with Shivaya for the eight count. Practice this for five minutes and then write about what happened for you when you added the mantra.

~~ Soften, allow... is an invitation to further drop any tension, any resistances, and to allow yourself to be as you are.... To be here softening, allowing unobstructed, fully as you are.... Bring your attention to your toes, breathe and soften, allow your toes to soften.... (Scan your whole body and whisper to each body area, soften... soften... allow, allow)

~~ As you soften and allow, with your exhale, try on the sound, ahhhhhhh, which opens the heart chakra. Again, deep inhale, exhale ahhhhhhhh.... Now, allow a deep inhale, and then next exhale, allow the sound, shhhhhhh... another way for calming and soothing... deep inhale, allow shhhhhhhh.... Notice what this does to your brain chemistry, I believe it changes it... Give yourself the gift of soothing... quieting.... Calming the mind... if you don't think your mind's thoughts are serving you, practice allowing this sound.... Deep inhale, shhhhhhhhh... soften, allow.

~~ Open.... Stand, open your arms wide, drop back and feel the opening or your heart and drop your head back and sound out. Open....

THE PRACTICE OF ENJOYMENT

Duration: {{ audioDuration804 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

As we breathe, soften, allow and open to the moment, enjoyment is more accessible.

When we stop resisting our realities and say yes instead, when we soften into what is, when we allow and open into the is-ness, we have greater capacity for enjoying the present moment.

The practice of naming your experience of enjoyment helps you to claim it. When you notice the enjoyment of your experience, the enjoyment expands.

Also, make it a practice to ask yourself often, “How can I enjoy this moment even more?” This invites you to make any shifts necessary (physically, mentally, emotionally) to help increase the enjoyment factor for you in any given moment. The invitation opens you to enjoy yourself and to tweak anything that can increase the flow of enjoyment. This way the practice of enjoyment is an art.

Do you love your life? Do you enjoy your life? If you do, claim it shamelessly. If you don't, make the necessary changes so that you do love and enjoy your life. It is a must.

PRACTICES FOR ENJOYING YOUR LIFE

~~ Practice naming at least five times in a day an experience you are enjoying. Breathe into it. Name it, claim it, notice what happens.

~~ Invite yourself at least five times in a day how you can enjoy this moment more.

~~ Do something that you love and enjoy doing. Give it to yourself and honor yourself for paying attention and being able to implement more enjoyment.

~~ Journal about the questions, “Do I love my life? Do I enjoy my life?” If the answer is yes, write about loving and enjoying your life. See what comes out on the page as you describe enjoying your life.

~~ If you are not loving your life, what needs to change? Write about it. Feelings are messengers that often call out for us to listen and sometimes make a shift. If they are uncomfortable feelings, ask yourself if it would serve you to either change your perception or change your behavior in some way or both? Write about it.

~~ As you practice the Commitment Mudra Flow, ask yourself what you need to do that would help you for it to be even more enjoyable to you. Would it be more enjoyable if you light a candle while you practice it, or if you stood while you practiced it or would it serve you to hold each posture longer or to add music. Experiment with different ways to enjoy it. And, ask yourself, “Do I enjoy participating in the Commitment Mudra Flow?” If so, claim it!
