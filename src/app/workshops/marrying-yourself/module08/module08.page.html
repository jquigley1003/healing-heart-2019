WELCOME TO EMBODYING GODDESS WISDOM

We are embarking on the Practice of various axioms. Practice means training, repetition, study, application, implementation, execution. Every moment is a new opportunity to practice. THE PRACTICE OF ENJOYMENT

Duration: {{ audioDuration804 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

As we breathe, soften, allow and open to the moment, enjoyment is more accessible.

When we stop resisting our realities and say yes instead, when we soften into what is, when we allow and open into the is-ness, we have greater capacity for enjoying the present moment.

The practice of naming your experience of enjoyment helps you to claim it. When you notice the enjoyment of your experience, the enjoyment expands.

Also, make it a practice to ask yourself often, “How can I enjoy this moment even more?” This invites you to make any shifts necessary (physically, mentally, emotionally) to help increase the enjoyment factor for you in any given moment. The invitation opens you to enjoy yourself and to tweak anything that can increase the flow of enjoyment. This way the practice of enjoyment is an art.

Do you love your life? Do you enjoy your life? If you do, claim it shamelessly. If you don't, make the necessary changes so that you do love and enjoy your life. It is a must.

Three Little Birds - Bob Marley & The Wailers

LIVING IN THE DHARMA

Duration: {{ audioDuration805 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

Dharma is a Sanskrit work that refers to the path of right action. The official definition of it is the eternal and inherent nature of reality, regarded in Hinduism as a cosmic law underlying right behavior and social order. Bottom line, this means that there are inherent virtues and guiding principles in everything we engage in, in every role we live. There is a dharma in being a daughter, a son, a spouse, a parent, a teacher, whatever you do — it is the alignment of thought and action with your values and principles. Dharma is how we show up... to our own principles and values, not in reaction to how others treat us, but rather our ability to respond based on our relationship to dharma. This calls upon the ability to pause, allow reactivity to pass through without acting on it and not fueling it, and then moving in the direction of love and respect with how we do respond.

Tara Brach addresses this beautifully in this talk:

Learning to Respond Not React - Tara Brach

PRACTICES FOR LIVING THE DHARMA

~~ Now, circle one of the ways you are living in alignment with dharma. Lets give you “a credit report” as my writing coach, Nina Hart calls it. A “credit report” is exactly that — giving yourself credit. Acknowledging yourself. Write a credit report on yourself for five minutes. Set your timer. Lets give you acknowledgement, credit, applause.

~~ Now, star one of the ways you are living off the mark, a way that screams out to you. Journal about how you might shift your behavior, your thoughts, your attitude, your choices so that you come more into alignment with your dharma in this situation. What is the adjustment you need to make here that would make a difference? Write about it for ten minutes and then commit to one action you can take that feels empowering to you. Set your timer.

~~ Act on what you committed to.

~~ Write about what your priorities are, what you hold as most important to you in life. Now, write about how you spend your time. Compare these lists. Do they match or are they off? Once again, what needs to be tweaked here? Write about this and star something that you want to execute immediately into action.
