WELCOME TO EMBODYING GODDESS WISDOM

We are embarking on the Practice of various axioms. Practice means training, repetition, study, application, implementation, execution. Every moment is a new opportunity to practice. If you blow it one moment, there is always the next moment, to practice. We are living into these gems, these axioms, these invitations.... Not perfectly, just practicing into them.

Practice is goddess wisdom. It is the blending of feminine and masculine energies. We are intentionally implementing and enacting a specific theme. This is our masculine in action. Our feminine energy is in how we are being with ourselves... compassionate, permission-giving, patient, and knowing that it is all a practice as we can be as messy and color outside of the lines to so speak as we practice.

There are a zillion axioms and goddess wisdom guideposts that we could be endeavoring into. So, the few that we will be practicing, doing it wholeheartedly. Dive into this. Make it yours. Allow excitement for each day to be richer because of the focus of the day.

My recommendation is that you either listen to the intro recording for each day's theme and you go through the days sequentially with each new axiom. And then you do it again, this time you dive into the exercises and songs and Ted talks that are offered.

PRACTICES FOR BEING PRESENT, NOW WOW

~~ Experiment with intensifying your senses. Choose one at a time and intensify it. Experience your world through the gift of this sense. PRACTICES FOR BREATHING, SOFTENING, ALLOWING, OPENING

~~ Practice going outside and truly breathing deep and long. Allow the inhale to be like powerful ingesting of Prana, and allow your exhale to be long and go all the way before you inhale. Every system in the body relies on oxygen. As you breathe deep and long, you allow for greater mental cognition and clarity, digestion, sleep, improved immunity response, and deeper sleep. Breathe deeper and longer, you are freer from stress. Breathing consciously and fully is one of the most loving things you can do for yourself. It is the beginning point for all else. It is the opening. It is the yes. It is the claiming of your birthright to live and take up space. It informs your system that you know you are worthy and have a right to drink of life energy fully and shamelessly. It is your willingness to be alive.

~~ Practice this 4-7-8 breathing technique for ten minutes. Set your timer. Then write about what you noticed about your experience. How do you practice 4-7-8?

1. Breathe in through your nose to the count of four.
2. Hold the breath to the count of seven.
3. Exhale through your mouth to the count of eight.

~~ Now, lets add the mantra to it and see how that is for you. With the four count inhale, allow an Om to resonate within. Hold for seven with Namah. Exhale with Shivaya for the eight count. Practice this for five minutes and then write about what happened for you when you added the mantra.

Calming the mind... if you don't think your mind's thoughts are serving you, practice allowing this sound.... Deep inhale, shhhhhhhhh... soften, allow.

THE PRACTICE OF ENJOYMENT

Duration: {{ audioDuration804 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

As we breathe, soften, allow and open to the moment, enjoyment is more accessible.

When we stop resisting our realities and say yes instead, when we soften into what is, when we allow and open into the is-ness, we have greater capacity for enjoying the present moment.

The practice of naming your experience of enjoyment helps you to claim it. When you notice the enjoyment of your experience, the enjoyment expands.

Also, make it a practice to ask yourself often, “How can I enjoy this moment even more?” This invites you to make any shifts necessary (physically, mentally, emotionally) to help increase the enjoyment factor for you in any given moment. The invitation opens you to enjoy yourself and to tweak anything that can increase the flow of enjoyment. This way the practice of enjoyment is an art.

Do you love your life? Do you enjoy your life? If you do, claim it shamelessly. If you don't, make the necessary changes so that you do love and enjoy your life. It is a must.

Three Little Birds - Bob Marley & The Wailers

PRACTICES FOR ENJOYING YOUR LIFE

~~ Practice naming at least five times in a day an experience you are enjoying. Breathe into it. Name it, claim it, notice what happens.

~~ Invite yourself at least five times in a day how you can enjoy this moment more.

~~ Do something that you love and enjoy doing. Give it to yourself and honor yourself for paying attention and being able to implement more enjoyment.

~~ Journal about the questions, “Do I love my life? Do I enjoy my life?” If the answer is yes, write about loving and enjoying your life. See what comes out on the page as you describe enjoying your life.

~~ If you are not loving your life, what needs to change? Write about it. Feelings are messengers that often call out for us to listen and sometimes make a shift. If they are uncomfortable feelings, ask yourself if it would serve you to either change your perception or change your behavior in some way or both? Write about it.

~~ As you practice the Commitment Mudra Flow, ask yourself what you need to do that would help you for it to be even more enjoyable to you. Would it be more enjoyable if you light a candle while you practice it, or if you stood while you practiced it or would it serve you to hold each posture longer or to add music. Experiment with different ways to enjoy it. And, ask yourself, “Do I enjoy participating in the Commitment Mudra Flow?” If so, claim it!

LIVING IN THE DHARMA

Duration: {{ audioDuration805 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

Dharma is a Sanskrit work that refers to the path of right action. The official definition of it is the eternal and inherent nature of reality, regarded in Hinduism as a cosmic law underlying right behavior and social order. Bottom line, this means that there are inherent virtues and guiding principles in everything we engage in, in every role we live. There is a dharma in being a daughter, a son, a spouse, a parent, a teacher, whatever you do — it is the alignment of thought and action with your values and principles. Dharma is how we show up... to our own principles and values, not in reaction to how others treat us, but rather our ability to respond based on our relationship to dharma. This calls upon the ability to pause, allow reactivity to pass through without acting on it and not fueling it, and then moving in the direction of love and respect with how we do respond.

PRACTICES FOR LIVING THE DHARMA

~~ Bring pen to paper and just start writing about your relationship with dharma. Where in your life do you you feel solidly in alignment with dharma? And where in your life do you feel most off the mark? Just write. Set your timer for fifteen minutes and see what comes out on your page.

~~ Now, circle one of the ways you are living in alignment with dharma. Lets give you “a credit report” as my writing coach, Nina Hart calls it. A “credit report” is exactly that — giving yourself credit. Acknowledging yourself. Write a credit report on yourself for five minutes. Set your timer. Lets give you acknowledgement, credit, applause.

~~ Now, star one of the ways you are living off the mark, a way that screams out to you. Journal about how you might shift your behavior, your thoughts, your attitude, your choices so that you come more into alignment with your dharma in this situation. What is the adjustment you need to make here that would make a difference? Write about it for ten minutes and then commit to one action you can take that feels empowering to you. Set your timer.

~~ Act on what you committed to.

~~ Write about what your priorities are, what you hold as most important to you in life. Now, write about how you spend your time. Compare these lists. Do they match or are they off? Once again, what needs to be tweaked here? Write about this and star something that you want to execute immediately into action.
