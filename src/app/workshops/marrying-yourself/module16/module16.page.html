INTENTIONS, COMMITMENTS, AND VOWS

Duration: {{ audioDuration1601 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

Intention is a wish or idea that you sincerely mean to carry out, whether you pull it off or not.

Intention can be subject to fluctuation and change according to what is happening...

Intention gives guidance and form to where and how you channel your energy.

Intention gives the power of presence to what you want to actualize.
It is empowering to state your intentions.

Commitment is the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause, activity or goal. (As you commit to marrying yourself, YOU are the cause, activity and goal!)

Commitment is a pledge, a dedication, held as obligation, a must, no matter what.
Commitment remains steady, no matter what you feel in a given moment.

Commitment calls upon the warrior and is infused with conviction — conviction to keep one's promise, one's pledge.

Commitment often goes hand-in-hand with action.
Commitment brings intention into action. Into manifestation. Into actualization.

Making a commitment to yourself and to another involves dedicating yourself.

A commitment obligates you to stand by the words you pledged.

A vow is a solemn promise, usually invoking a divine witness, regarding your future acts or behavior.
A vow is an oath, a dedication to a specific person, activity or cause. And that is the “I, myself, know. I, myself, trust. I, myself, love. I am my beloved, my beloved is me.”

This writing module is all about you preparing to pledge to yourself a binding commitment to make yourself your own home — your primary beloved relationship for the rest of your life.

GUIDANCE ON HOW TO WRITE VOWS

Duration: {{ audioDuration1602 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

Your ability to follow through and live into each and every one of your commitments, vows and intentions will ultimately either powerfully build your trust in yourself or brutally hurt your relationship with yourself.

Lynn on Writing Her Vows

Assignment: Begin Writing Your Vows

Beloved ________, (your name), I commit to you....

I vow to you...

My intention is...

Don't think about it, just write, and write.... And breathe...
Then, read out loud what you wrote and see how it lands.
Ask yourself if what you wrote is true and if you could make it more honest in some way — to make sure you can and will live true to what you declare to yourself in your wedding ceremony.

This is a deeply profound and important time...

How you show up in this matters.

Go deep. And write...
