The Antidote to Codependency

Ah, So This is Codependency

Ah, so this is codependency!

Unknowing of even the word, I was immersed in my addiction ~~.
Codependency.

I started learning about “codependency” as years of self-neglect and self-betrayal that caught up to me.

Codependency ~~
An excessive emotional, physical, and psychological reliance on a dysfunctional relationship.

One’s sense of identity to based on another.

The codependent refuses to acknowledge that there is a problem inside of themselves.
The codependent actively acts out the belief that one’s own needs should be sacrificed in service to others.
The codependent is driven to help, regardless of the consequences to oneself.
Because it arises from trying to avoid one’s own feelings and needs. And its an attempt to try to get one’s own needs met. It just doesn’t work

“I will try to fix you — I will focus on you so that I don’t have to feel what I am feeling. If I can change you, then I won’t feel this way anymore.”

I will give my all to you so that you will get better and then I will feel worthy or significant or safe or like you won’t leave me.

Although the codependent might appear to be so giving and thoughtful, one’s own needs for recognition and validation as well as fears of abandonment are perpetuating the behaviors.

Underlying the continual efforts to rescue the other are unrelenting desires to be needed, loved and cherished.

Also underlying the codependent behaviors is not knowing how to be with one’s own feelings and to self-soothe.

Codependency is a relationship in which one party, usually the individual who uses, has many physical and emotional needs, and the other party, a family member or friend, devotes most of their time and energy trying to fulfill those needs. The second party is referred to as the codependent. Oftentimes, the codependent spends so much time and energy assisting the loved one that the codependent’s own life, activities, and relationships suffer in the process.

This behavioral condition can be detrimental to both parties involved in the relationship. The codependent’s individual life will suffer and the loved one might have a more difficult time with recovery and maintaining a life away from destructive, unhealthy behaviors. Usually, a codependent individual is overly attentive and can enable the challenges faced by the loved one, making the recovery journey more complicated.

There are recognizable symptoms of a codependent person. These include:

  • Believing they can care for themselves without any help
  • Low self-esteem
  • Seeking recognition and praise from others
  • Difficulty setting or maintaining healthy boundaries
  • Feeling a need to be a caretaker for others
  • Remaining extremely loyal, even in harmful situations
  • Obsession with relationships, even if an emotional connection is lacking
  • A need to please others and ensure others have a positive opinion of themselves
  • Poor communication
  • Difficulty handling change
  • Often doing more than their share of work/responsibilities

Because of the low self-esteem that codependent individuals have, they may look for some kind of activity to keep themselves engaged and make themselves feel better. When a codependent person takes on the role of caretaker for someone going through addiction, the intentions are good. But eventually, the desire to take care of this person becomes a compulsion.

Everyone has their story. What is your story? When and with whom did you live out your codependency? Or, you may presently be involved in a codependent relationship. Either way, bring your pen to paper, set your timer for ten to fifteen minutes and write. Just write and see what comes out.

Now read what you wrote. Breathe and notice what stirs in your body. Bring the palms of your hands to your body where you feel the sensations and breathe into your heart, through your hands and into where your body needs to feel the love...

Self-Betrayal

Self-betrayal. Hurts. So deep. And I felt so angry. An anger I hadn’t allowed myself to feel before.

Really, at myself, for abandoning myself. I didn’t know it, but by showing up for another so intensely, and trying to avoid being abandoned, I was seriously being abandoned. By my-self. By me!

Have you experienced betraying yourself? Breathe. Are there any areas in your current life where you are not living in alignment with yourself and are in self-betrayal? Set a timer again for fifteen minutes. Bring your pen to paper and just write. You can start with “I know about self-betrayal...”. And just see what comes out on the page....

Now read what you wrote. See where you feel this is in your body and again, bring your hands to your body and breathe love into yourself.

Retrieving Myself

...more retrieving myself

This woke me to the knowingness that I had no choice ~~
but to retrieve myself.

The earth plates in my life were shifting - from the focus on “The Other” to myself.

It became clear that no one but myself could fill the aching hole and this ultimately was so empowering and transformational.

I had to find the courage to actually face who I had become, how my sense of self had deteriorated and how I was devastated by all the ways I betrayed myself, big and small, loud and hidden.

The necessity to retrieve myself became my number one mission - to listen to myself, to feel my feelings, to become aware of my needs, to learn how to advocate for myself, to voice what I need, value and believe in, to stand up for myself and say “no” when I mean “no” and “yes” when I mean “yes,” to have boundaries and to protect my energy and the younger parts of myself, and... to practice self-care as a necessity.... all of this became internal parts of this enlivening, deepening journey that led me to marry myself.

Retrieving....
Me, myself and I.
A foundation with myself that no one and no-thing could ever diminish...

Committing to the recovery of codependency is the process of retrieving myself.

Recovery is releasing trying to change someone and instead being willing to be with how I feel in the presence of how someone actually is with themselves and me — and then deciding if that works for me or not, if I am not going to change them.

Recovery is having boundaries and attending to what is arising inside of myself as priority — my own feelings and needs — and learning how to advocate for what I need.

Recovery is not looking for someone else to need and want me in order for me to feel okay.

Recovery is the willingness to be uncomfortable if I am not trying to fill the empty spaces.

Recovery is getting real with who others are versus who I want them to be.

Recovery is no longer giving to someone else based on their “potential” but rather accepting what is so and getting clear on what I need and want — and to be willing to walk away if necessary.

Recovery is shifting my attention away from rescuing another to attending to myself — and learning how to receive how much I can give!

How to STOP Betraying Yourself and Start Loving You

What is a Marriage Where There is no Option for Divorce?

I began to contemplate,
What would be a marriage in which there was no divorce.

I knew immediately that the only true answer was that the only marriage where there is no option for divorce is a marriage to myself. I knew that moment that the remedy to this painful, painful experience of divorce was to ultimately marry myself.

Yup, I needed to marry me!

At first there I felt terrified to be alone ~~ and to not play out my old patterns of focusing on others, helping others, being there for others.

I chose to spend time with me and not others, even if it meant I was crying, journaling, wailing and feeling lonely. It was time to burn through all that by feeling it and communing with my—self.

As I allowed myself to feel it all, I sourced a strength and comfort within me.

In time, I grew to love and enjoy and even crave time with myself. I got the knack and the must for self-care. It became a necessity.

Time for your own self-exploration. Write the words, "Self-retrieval". And then just write.... Perhaps you have experienced something similar to me or perhaps there are parts of you that are calling out to be retrieved now. Just write for ten to fifteen minutes. Don’t think about it. Just bring pen to paper and see what comes out on the page.

Now read what you wrote. See where this is in your body and bring your hands to your body and again, breathe into your heart, into the palms of your hands, having contact with your body, and breathe in the love, receive, and breathe the love out... let it be a circular movement of inhaling and exhaling, a channel of love.

Blossoming Into Myself

This relationship with myself began to truly root so deep inside my core and became so alive - so dynamic, so core, so key - and still is.

And what I love so much is that no one and no thing can take it away! I have said this before and I will say it again.

Because it is so great. So freeing. So empowering. So true.

No one can diminish it!

It is in my bones.

I got engaged. I wooed and romanced myself the way I longed for. I brought me flowers and an engagement ring. I gave myself kindness — appreciations, acknowledgements, sensual time, fun time, gifts, love notes, protection and boundaries. I looked myself in the mirror and I asked myself if I would one day marry me? I told myself - in the mirror - eye to eye - that we still had a lot of healing and growing to do before we could actually take vows, but I was committing to learning how to be number one priority for myself. I was committed to doing this healing work within myself so that when the time came for my wedding vows, I could truly say and feel that I am my beloved and my beloved is me.

Vertical Alignment

The practice of Vertical Alignment came to me during my engagement time and remains an antidote to “horizontal relating.” Horizontal relating is based in codependent behaviors. It’s all about caring about how you see me, if you like me, how you respond to me as defining and impacting my own self-esteem. Vertical alignment is the opposite. It is the rooting in the earth and the rising into the sky. It is the act of stepping into the portal of energy, of light, that is above, moves through you and roots deep into the earth. The practice of Vertical Alignment has the power to transform you energetically, saving you from draining mental gymnastics that try to shift you out of the codependent undertow.

Try it on. Step into it. Let it re-arrange you.

Marrying Myself

After a year of separation and right after my divorce was finalized, actually, the very same day, I flew to India. I married myself in my last week of my stay at the ashram. I was ready. I remember. I stood tall, by the Goddess Durga statue, draped in a lovely soft flowing dress, with my vows in hand. I remember — I tuned into sacred vertical energies and mindfully spoke each vow to myself out loud... before the ancient ones, the invisible ones, and the presence of the sacred.

I committed to myself as I received each vow and each vow landed profoundly in my soul.

I knew then... and I have known every day since then (for thirty plus years) that there is no divorce and I am in a sacred beloved primary relationship with myself. At all times.

Marrying myself was the antidote to my codependency patterns that had contributed to ending my marriage with my husband and it was the beginning of the rest of my life. It was my turning point. I became my true beloved — and I was never to be the same again... and it is wonderful. Solid. Empowering. Truly, like nothing else.

I love being married to myself. I have never regretted it. I have never doubted it. It is one of the best things I have ever done for myself.

So I brought it home from India with me. I lived it. My marriage. To myself. Ahhhhh.... No divorce. Being married to myself made everything different ~~ Dating, actually all relationships, my self-regard, my internal experience every day and night.

I had to share it. Because whatever I find good in life I want to share with others. I offered the Marrying Yourself experience as a weekend retreat. We did a deep dive but a quick one. The twenty women married themselves at the close of the weekend. They loved it. I knew it fell short. I knew it wasn’t it. I knew how much richer it could be. How my experience of being engaged for nearly a year covered so much more ground, lets put it that way. I came to terms with that there really is a reason for long engagement periods and that for now on, the Marrying Yourself Journey would include an engagement period lasting for nearly a year or more. To allow for the work. Because I knew how much focus, how much work, how much sweat, how much burning, how much discovery, how much nurturing and persisting it took. Since I realized that others also needed this time for growing into being ready to marry — And that’s how its been since and its been amazing....

It is a privilege to be present and witness others look deep into their own eyes in a mirror, making vows to themselves and putting a ring on their own fingers. Likewise, its powerful to be witnessed making vows to yourself and feeling the support surround you as you make vows to yourself in the mirror. Although this is an online course, we will orchestrate it so that you will have the opportunity if you want to be witnessed when it is time for you to marry yourself.

It is also a privilege to be in this journey with you because every step of it matters. The journey is even more important than the arrival because the arrival is just the natural outcome of how you do this journey with yourself. The magic is in how you show up to yourself during this journey.

Each of us has our own story. The story that leads us to discovering the cracks in our foundations and then the necessity for healing and filling in these cracks. With self-love cement.

So, close your eyes and bring honor to yourself for doing this. For taking time to listen to this and to reflect on yourself.

In a moment, I invite you to open your eyes and bring your pen to paper and write... I am embarking on this journey toward marrying myself. I want to marry myself.... And just start writing... Ten minutes.

Now read what you wrote. See where this is in your body and bring your hands to your body and breathe love into yourself.
