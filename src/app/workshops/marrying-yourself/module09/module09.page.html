Assignment: Self-Reflection Exercise Two

SELF-REFLECTION EXERCISE TWO

Duration: {{ audioDuration0907 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

The next exercise is to help us get honest about what is so for ourselves. It is an experiment of being open to honestly try it all on — trying on the traits that you listed for each person.

Sit in front of a mirror if you are up for it. As you go through your lists, make eye contact with yourself with each one. Otherwise just sit with yourself in a quiet place.

This is how we will do this exercise.

You will be going through the lists of attributes (positive and negative) that are on your lists. (Ignore the people's names they are associated with right now). Right now, we are looking at your own relationship with each of these qualities that you have certainly spent time around.

You will try each attribute on by saying: "I am ________."
(Say the first attribute, say the sentence two times. Try it on.)

On a scale from one to ten, ten being totally true and one being not true at all, rate how true the statement resonates for you. Be as honest as possible. Pay attention to your body. Also, visit seeing yourself from other people's eyes and how others experience you. Go with whatever number arises for you.

As you go through each one, record your rating with the attribute in your journal.

If your rating is five or more for an attribute, look in the mirror if you can or close your eyes and be with yourself as you repeat the sentence, "I am ________" and then, add to your statement: "This is part of my _________(positive or shadow) feminine/masculine aspect, and my intention is to accept and embrace all of who I am", and then allow a deep breath.

Assignment: Self-Reflection Exercise Three

SELF-REFLECTION EXERCISE THREE

Duration: {{ audioDuration0908 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

Now let's ask these questions and write down a few things:

1. What attributes were rated five and up?

2. Star two of the positive attributes about yourself that right now really speak to you.

3. Now, do the same for two shadow attributes of yourself that jump out to you that you want to prioritize and bring attention to.

4. Now, invite each attribute that you chose to speak to you directly. Take pen to paper and begin with, "I am your _________." (And write the attribute.) E.g. "I am your generosity." and then see what this attribute wants to say to you. Perhaps it wants to tell you how it plays out in your life, what it does for you, what it costs you.

For example, let's say you starred self-absorption. Self-absorption might say, "I am your self-absorption. I am operating even when you don't know it. I am really most interested in my own experience. That is why I usually dominate conversations and I don't like to hear others talk as much as I like to talk. I don't ask others questions, because I really want others to hear me. Anyway, that's how I make myself known in your life that I am aware of right now. I am trying to feel loved but I think I push people away. I think I cost you friends."

Another example: "I am your generosity. I love to be generous with others in your life. I love to be generous with my time, energy and money to the four-leggeds and the people in our life. With being so generous, I help you to feel goodness. I can get carried away with being so generous with others that I forget about your needs. This creates burn out. I need to make you priority as much as others or more so! I like thinking about being generous with you, my-self!"

5. Look over the list and notice what aspects of your masculine energy need mellowing?

6. Choose one masculine attribute that needs mellowing and come up with a strategy on how to go about turning down the volume on this attribute. For example, let's say the attribute to domineering. You experience yourself giving your opinion and advice without being asked for it. You might put into practice pausing before you give advice or your opinion and finding out what someone else thinks first and then asking if they want to hear your advice.

7. Look over the list and choose one masculine attribute that you want to strengthen.

8. How might you go about strengthening this masculine attribute? E.g. Let's say you chose, directness. You might put into practice being direct with what you feel and what you need to people close to you.

9. Choose an aspect of your feminine that needs more more loving attention, protection and support and a way to implement this. For example, receptivity. You might need to make it a practice to shield yourself because you are so open and receptive to other people's energies.

10. What feminine attribute needs strengthening and what is one way to do this? For example, let's say softening is something you want to prioritize. You might want to make it a practice to whisper to yourself, "It's okay to soften, soften.... Shhhhh, soften..., it's safe to soften."

METTA MEDITATION

Duration: {{ audioDuration705 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

The metta prayer of loving kindness is a prayer or mantra used as part of a loving-kindness meditation, or metta bhavana. This kind of meditation and use of a mantra is popular in the Buddhist tradition. The word, metta, comes from Pali and can be translated as "good will" or "loving kindness."

I am changing the words to fit our self retrieval theme. We will sit in meditation for this.

May I be safe and protected
May I be healthy and strong
May I be released from grudges, resentments and anger
May I be whole, retrieved, and embraced by forgiveness and love

THE GUEST HOUSE by RUMI

Duration: {{ audioDuration706 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

The Guest House

by Rumi

This being human is a guest house.
Every morning a new arrival.

A joy, a depression, a meanness,
some momentary awareness comes
As an unexpected visitor.

Welcome and entertain them all!
Even if they're a crowd of sorrows,
who violently sweep your house
empty of its furniture,
still treat each guest honorably.
He may be clearing you out
for some new delight.

The dark thought, the shame, the malice,
meet them at the door laughing,
and invite them in.

Be grateful for whoever comes,
because each has been sent
as a guide from beyond.

THE INVITATION by ORIAH MOUNTAIN DREAMER

Duration: {{ audioDuration707 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

The Invitation

by Oriah Mountain Dreamer

It doesn't interest me what you do for a living. I want to know what you ache for and if you dare to dream of meeting your heart's longing.

It doesn't interest me how old you are. I want to know if you will risk looking like a fool for love, for your dream, for the adventure of being alive.

It doesn't interest me what planets are squaring your moon. I want to know if you have touched the centre of your own sorrow, if you have been opened by life's betrayals or have become shrivelled and closed from fear of further pain.

I want to know if you can sit with pain, mine or your own, without moving to hide it, or fade it, or fix it.

I want to know if you can be with joy, mine or your own; if you can dance with wildness and let the ecstasy fill you to the tips of your fingers and toes without cautioning us to be careful, be realistic, remember the limitations of being human.

It doesn't interest me if the story you are telling me is true. I want to know if you can disappoint another to be true to yourself. If you can bear the accusation of betrayal and not betray your own soul. If you can be faithless and therefore trustworthy.

I want to know if you can see Beauty even when it is not pretty every day. And if you can source your own life from its presence.

I want to know if you can live with failure, yours and mine, and still stand at the edge of the lake and shout to the silver of the full moon, 'Yes.'

It doesn't interest me to know where you live or how much money you have. I want to know if you can get up after the night of grief and despair, weary and bruised to the bone and do what needs to be done to feed the children.

It doesn't interest me who you know or how you came to be here. I want to know if you will stand in the centre of the fire with me and not shrink back.

It doesn't interest me where or what or with whom you have studied. I want to know what sustains you from the inside when all else falls away.

I want to know if you can be alone with yourself and if you truly like the company you keep in the empty moments.
