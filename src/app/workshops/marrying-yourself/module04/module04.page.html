Bring true curiosity and interest to...

your self....

To knowing — you.

To clarifying who you are — to yourself.

To naming who you know yourself to be.

To experiencing breath — breath breathing into your own being.... And out

Landing Safely

by Wren Breen

I'm wrong, I'm bad, I don't belong,
Became my twisted Siren's song,
And haunted by the melody,
I burned myself in effigy.
The darkness once denied within,
Became my one and only friend.

My fractured vessel sunk down deep,
A depth at which no light could reach.
The light remained, but far away,
Safe from the bottom where I lay.
All connection severed clean,
Apart from knowing what I'd seen:

A love that permeated all,
A Grace I'd find had caught my fall.
I did not know this Grace back then,
With charred remains and tattered ends.
But by and by, I'd come to see,
How all this pain would work for me.

I scratched and clawed for months and years,
Just surviving, trapped in fear.
No North Star shined, nor beacon blinked,
But every now and then, a wink.
A nudge that kept me moving on,
A thought, “perhaps, not all is gone.”

While time has not healed all my wounds,
It's lead to corridors and rooms,
Of seeking souls, the same as I,
Who don't demand an alibi.
Just gentle glances, lended ears,
Suggestions that there's more than fear.

And now I take the best parts back,
Leaving shame, and doubt, and lack.
The gift of fire becoming clear,
My form, in love, draws ever near.
I know there was no other way,
To fill the shoes I wear today.

The medicine of trauma healed,
Perhaps there is no thing more real.
The lessons gleaned formed into pearls,
I carry now into the world.
Landing safely in my skin,
Learning how to live again.

THE "I AM..." WALKING PRACTICES FOLLOWED BY A WRITING EXERCISE

Duration: {{ audioDuration0404 * 1000 | date:'mm:ss' }}

~~ Take a slow meditative walk outside. As you walk, allow yourself to look through the lens of clear, crisp vision. Practice seeing everything clearly and vividly that your gaze falls upon. And as your eyes look upon what is before you, experiment with saying out loud, “I am ____________” and fill it in with whatever you are looking at. For example:

“I am e.g. the cherry blossom tree.”
“ I am the dog that is running after the ball.”

Just try it on and see what arises. Does it have truth particles for you? Does it feel foreign? Familiar? Easy? Challenging? Do you feel like crying? Do you want to laugh? Are you feeling expansive — or contracted? Lifted or grounded?

~~ After you do this for fifteen minutes, sit with yourself, with your journal and for ten minutes again write, I am... and see what comes out on the paper now that you have done this practice.

~~ When you are done, read it to yourself.

~~ Now, turn the page. Get some color pencils or markers or crayons and draw a picture of yourself on the next page of your journal. Give yourself permission for it to be messy and terrible and sloppy and abstract and childlike. Just draw your you-ness as you feel you in this moment.
