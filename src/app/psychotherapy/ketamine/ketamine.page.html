Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy

Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy is the name that I have given to a dynamic, experiential approach to healing that takes you beyond just "talking about" the situations and stressors in your life. This method engages your body/heart/mind/spirit in experiential processes that help you to embody the changes you are growing into.

Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy offers a format to re-work your internalized scripting by enacting it with the help of other people or objects enrolled as parts of your psyche or life circumstances. These enactments provide opportunities to reconstruct the original, historic blueprint from family of origin dynamics. By doing so, there is a freeing up from old wiring of past conditioning and beliefs along with a re-wiring of new imprints for healthy emotional experiences. The result is a foundational transformation. Integrative Self Enactments are most effective in group settings,* but can also be utilized in individual sessions.

*Group Settings -- Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy generally take place in a closed circle of no more than nine people. The groups meet weekly and call for an ongoing commitment from all group participants. The group offers a sense of community (which I often refer to as “tribe”) in service to the work of personal and interpersonal transformation.
