More About Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy opens the door for us to explore what we truly feel and think; but even more importantly, psychotherapy helps us to learn how to respond to ourselves with more compassion and curiosity, rather than judgment or self-avoidance.

I approach psychotherapy as a sacred contract between therapist and client. Within the sanctum of our relationship, I invite you to learn how to be courageously honest with yourself – which includes recognizing and re-connecting with your inner strengths and capabilities.

Though deep listening is foundational, I take an active role in offering feedback and teaching you tools for shifting your inner and outer realties.

I aim to help you to learn how to listen to your body, to feel more deeply into your own true heart, and to train your mind to be your ally -- rather than your enemy.

My desire is to make every session count, so that you consistently leave with something new to work with. My intention is that with each meeting, your self-awareness deepens, your “tool box” expands and you grow to feel freer to express your authentic self.

We are not meant to do this by ourselves -- we never were.
