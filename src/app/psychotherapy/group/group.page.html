Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy

I see experiential work in a group therapy setting as one of the most effective psychotherapeutic approaches for healing.

At the very least in a group, people experience not being alone in how they feel. There is an opportunity to receive unconditional acceptance and support as well as a safe container to do deep transformative work.

Transformational Integrative Group Psychotherapy is rooted in psychomotor therapy. Along with a blend of other body-oriented therapies, the aim is to facilitate fundamental and lasting changes in one's inner blueprint by offering a corrective emotional parenting imprinting. This involves enrollments that antidote the specific dysfunctional "inner wiring" from childhood by imprinting new and more inherently satisfying emotional learning experiences.
